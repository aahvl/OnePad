## Hi! This is my journal on how I built OnePad. Enjoy reading about the process!

### Phase 1: Schematic & PCB Design (Days 1 to 3)
To start off OnePad, I spent the first three days diving into the schematic and PCB layout. It took multiple revisions to get it right; my initial design was much too tall vertically, and I wasnt happy with the form factor. This was my inital design below!
<img width="256" height="511" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/271a4b98-960c-466d-acd9-04060ef660e1" />

During this phase, I learned how to properly utilize global labels to keep the schematic clean, as well as how to daisy-chain LEDs for the backlighting.

### Phase 2: Casing & 3D Modeling (Days 4 to 6)
With the PCB dimensions finalized, I moved on to designing the enclosure. This required learning my way around CAD tools like Shelling, Extruding, and Filleting to create a clean, functional case.
One of the coolest challenges was learning how to import a .dxf file to embed the OneKey logo directly into the design. It took a lot of trial and error to get the tolerances right and figure out what would fit nicely with the case—especially when designing the custom LED grill holes to ensure the lighting diffused perfectly.

Also, a big thanks to Maxstellar for giving me ideas on how to make it look better!
